Plan your content calendar by scheduling posts in advance. When connected to Facebook, Market Trak publishes them automatically at your chosen time.

## Scheduling a post

When creating or editing a post:

1. Instead of publishing immediately, click **Schedule**
2. Pick a date and time
3. Confirm the schedule

Your post will appear in your content calendar and publish automatically at the scheduled time.

### How scheduling works

- A background job checks for scheduled posts and publishes them when the time arrives
- Posts are published to all connected platforms (currently Facebook)
- You receive a notification when the post is successfully published
- If publishing fails (e.g. expired token), you will be notified so you can retry

## Content calendar

The Social Posts page shows your upcoming and past posts in a calendar view. Each day shows:

- Scheduled posts waiting to go out
- Published posts with their status
- Platform indicators (Facebook, Instagram)

This gives you a visual overview of your content cadence, so you can spot gaps and plan ahead.

## Publishing to Facebook

To publish directly to Facebook, you need to connect your Facebook account first. See [Facebook Integration](/help-center/integrations/facebook) for setup instructions.

Once connected:

1. Create your post as normal
2. Choose **Publish Now** or **Schedule**
3. The post, including image and caption, is sent directly to your Facebook page

### Video posts

Market Trak supports video content for social posts. You can upload a video when editing a post, and it will be published alongside your caption. Video upload is available in the post editor, not during the initial creation flow.

## Downloading for manual posting

If you prefer to post manually (e.g. to Instagram, which does not have direct API publishing), you can:

1. Create your post in Market Trak
2. Click **Download** to save the image
3. Copy the caption text
4. Post it manually on your chosen platform

This lets you use Market Trak as your content creation studio while posting wherever you like.

## Post statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | Saved but not scheduled or published |
| **Scheduled** | Set to publish at a future date/time |
| **Published** | Successfully posted to Facebook |
| **Failed** | Publishing attempt failed (check your Facebook connection) |

## Tips

- Schedule posts for when your audience is most active (typically mornings and early evenings)
- Maintain a consistent posting rhythm, aim for at least 2-3 posts per week
- Use the calendar view to identify gaps in your schedule
- Mix data-driven posts (market updates) with property-specific posts for variety
