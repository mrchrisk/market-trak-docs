---
title: Welcome Emails
description: Automatically send a welcome email to new leads when they are captured.
---

Welcome emails are sent automatically when a new lead is captured in your workspace. They make a great first impression and let leads know who you are and what to expect.

## How it works

When a lead enters your workspace (from a report lead gate, valuation form, or manual entry), Market Trak can automatically send them a welcome email. This happens:

1. **On lead creation** - for manually added leads or CSV imports (if the welcome email toggle is on)
2. **After email verification** - for leads that go through the email confirmation flow

The welcome email is sent as a one-off message, not as part of a campaign.

## Enabling or disabling

You can toggle welcome emails on or off in **Workspace Settings** under the **Email** section.

- **On** - every new lead receives a welcome email automatically
- **Off** - no automatic emails are sent (you can still send campaigns manually)

## The welcome email template

Your workspace has a system-provided welcome email template. This template:

- Is pre-written with sensible defaults
- Uses basic personalisation variables
- Includes a required unsubscribe link for compliance

### Available variables

The welcome email supports a limited set of safe variables:

| Variable | What it inserts |
|---|---|
| `{{lead_name}}` | Lead's first name |
| `{{location}}` | The location associated with the lead |
| `{{workspace_name}}` | Your company name |
| `{{unsubscribe_link}}` | Unsubscribe link (required) |

> **Note:** Report-specific variables (like `{{report_link}}` or `{{averagePrice}}`) are not available in welcome emails because the email is sent before a report context exists.

## Email delivery

Welcome emails are sent via **Mailgun** using your workspace's configured email domain. If you have a custom email domain set up, the welcome email comes from your domain. Otherwise, it uses the default Market Trak sending domain.

## Tips

- Keep your welcome email short and friendly
- Introduce your agency and what the lead can expect to receive
- Include your contact details so they know how to reach you
- The welcome email sets the tone for your relationship with the lead, make it count
